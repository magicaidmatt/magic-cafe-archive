I don't understand why signing a deal with Murphy's, Penguin, or VI would prevent Dan from posting an update here or emailing people who have already purchased his Masterworks project. One way to reassure customers could be to establish a deal with a reputable publisher like VI and have them make an announcement. Sadly, many people, some of whom purchased this product from him, seem to have lost all faith in his promises.

I admire Dan and his magic, but I am relieved I didn't back this project. Something raised my suspicions when he first released the details; if you need motivation to write your life's work, you should get an advance from a publisher. Seeing someone I hold in such high regard let so many people down is very disappointing.

I've had similar experiences on Kickstarter, although at least on that platform, you're aware that you might not receive your rewards. This was a purchased product, so I find all Dan's excuses very thin indeed! If he's struggling with his mental health, then I'd encourage him to contact all of his customers with an honest update and then rethink his approach to this project. I'm sure plenty of people in the magic industry would help him sort out this mess.

I truly hope that Dan receives the help he needs and that his customers get what they have been promised. Dave Forrest had a similar experience, which I mentioned in my Ruseletter a few months ago (see Monthly Update #7). I also discussed (via email) with Aaron Fisher the four-year delay of the 20th anniversary edition of The Paper Engine; he concurred that one of the biggest mistakes he made was the lack of transparent communication regarding the difficulties he faced with the project (see CC Club Playing Cards on Kickstarter for details). It is surprising how forgiving customers will be if you keep them updated and you're honest with them!
