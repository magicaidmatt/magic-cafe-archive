Pre-orders are so often a bad choice for the buyer. The repeated delays, the frustration, the lack of communication, the broken promises. But it does appear in some cases pre-orders may also be very bad for the seller, de-incentivizing them, re-enforcing unproductive behaviors, perpetuating undesirable results.

I do feel bad for Dan. You never know what struggles a person privately faces. Sometimes people are struggling more than they let on.

At the same time, I do agree with what davidpaul$ wrote above. When a person is struggling financially, giving them more money may seem kind in the moment, but it may end up being counter-productive. Of course it helps in the short term, but it doesn't address the root cause. I knew a woman who was gifted a home outright because she was struggling. A band new home given to her free and clear. No mortgage payment. And you know what happened? She ended up losing the house because she didn't pay the taxes. Because she never got her life on track financially.

Like Slim, I'm praying for Dan and his family. I hope and pray whatever issues he is facing, he is able to overcome them and get back on track.

Pre-orders are a bad idea, for everyone concerned.
