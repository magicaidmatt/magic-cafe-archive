As you all know, this is a new process for me. Yes, I've written and illustrated in the past... The Minotaur, lecture notes, my instructions, and so on... but I've never tackled a book (let alone, 3 books!). So, I'm taking a little extra time. As an artist, I'm never quite 100 percent satisfied with anything I produce. However, there comes a time when I just have to let go. Also, this is a big project. I've done big projects in the past as well, and always completed them. I'm always wary when I introduce a subscription project. I want the roll-out to go smoothly. I know there will be some glitches in the system... and there already have been. I know, I've just got to get this thing actually going and let you all see what I've got. Thank you for your patience and support up to this point. My birthday is coming up (Oct 14) and that seems like an appropriate time to give life to the project.
There. I've set a firm deadline.
And, I'm pretty sure the retail price of the books will increase at some point before all three are printed. So, subscribers have locked-in their already discounted price for the entire run. Of course, anyone reading this (who is not already a subscriber) can sign up to get the same benefit...

I have received an initial sampler of the Masterworks and it is just a double page spread in .pdf as promised but if it is indicative of the design quality of future installments, then I think most will be satisfied with the appearance which shows a lot of thought in the layout and design.

I do not think it appropriate to comment on the actual content outside of a closed forum for actual subscribers; it does deal with a bit of business which Dan places in the context of his early development in magic from his school days. But it reminds me favorably of the style of the best Einhorn books with a injection of some personal connection and history but not too much.

I hope this is a good indicator of the direction and style that will characterize this project and I expect the sophistication of the content will increase as well.

I look forward to the next installment.
