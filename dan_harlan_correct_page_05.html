I must admit, I'm feeling a bit hesitant to sneak back into this conversation. Please don't throw anything at me. It's been ages since I last posted. I haven't even had a chance to check-in at the Cafe for quite a while. Honestly, I'm embarrassed about my lengthy absence, and I know I owe all of you an explanation. Simply put, my life has been a whirlwind, to say the least. But I can hear you scream: "Where's my MasterWorks!?" I truly apologize for not keeping in touch with those of you who have placed your trust in me regarding this important project. I hope you can forgive my absence. I've been navigating my own challenges, but I realize I should be better at letting you know what's happening. Please rest assured, MasterWorks is still very much alive! I'm actively working on it, although it has encountered a few bumps along the road... something that shouldn’t come as a shock... but I really felt I was going to be able to overcome life's challenges with more aplomb.

I know there's nothing I can say that will correct my current situation. I'm behind schedule. Nothing can change that. Also, I know you're not interested in excuses, but you're likely curious about why progress has been slower than expected and what has caused the delays. Here we go...

I had always intended to dedicate much more time to MasterWorks. Sadly, the initial response was a bit of a letdown. No offense to all of you who signed up -- you are the reason I'm as far along as I am! A huge, heartfelt "Thank YOU!" to all my subscribers. I know you're struggling to stay positive and I haven't helped. I swear to you... your patience will be rewarded. My results have always been worth the wait. However, as I said, I was anticipating (and preparing for) a response that was at least five times bigger than what I actually got. Because I was unable to raise the required capital investment, I've had to diversify. Naturally, I have bills to pay, just like everyone else. And as any magician will tell you, making a living in this field is no walk in the park. Essentially, I received only enough initial income to support myself and my family's basic needs for about a year. So, I’ve had to keep touring and accept other gigs to make ends meet. I've even considered getting a "normal" job. Yes, it's been a struggle.

Also, as you may have heard, I now have a computer which is better equipped for my needs. My old computer was not up to the challenge to handle all the files and layout I've been putting together. After all, MasterWorks is 54 years of my life! A conservative estimate of 300 gigs and growing each day. I was constantly frustrated with the sluggish pace and intermittent crashes, but I just didn't have any extra money to spend. More accurately, I tend to put my needs last, and I just kept pushing on through the frustration.

Most embarrassingly, I've been wrestling with some feelings of depression and a nagging sense of inadequacy. I hope you realize how uncomfortable it makes me to admit all of this. That's why it has taken me so long to share. Ultimately, my lack of time, money, and motivation does not excuse me from fulfilling my promise to you. I chose to take on all this work, and I am committed to seeing it through, regardless of whether anyone cares or not. I genuinely hope it will be beneficial to everyone who owns it. I often do big projects... not because they'll be easy... rather, I do it because I believe in contributing to help others. My time here is limited and I'd like to spend it positively influencing the future.

So, I humbly ask my current subscribers to extend me a bit more grace and patience. You deserve my best and I simply have to correct my course in order to deliver it. I also realize the only way I can generate excitement and interest in MasterWorks is to have more finished pages to show. I have enlisted the services of a co-writer/collaborator who will help me stick to a regular schedule. My family is aware of my struggles and are incredibly supportive. The rest is up to me...

www.themystictower.com
